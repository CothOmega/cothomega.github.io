Emotional Conflict

Conflict is something I tend to seek to avoid. My nature tends to have me be the first one to step back and try to evaluate why a confrontation is even happening. The last conflict I had with someone was my fiance just a week ago. I was stressing out about my upcoming move to New York for Dev Bootcamp, and was isolating myself pretty hard. I was focused on the uncertainties of the future vs the Now. She didn't appreciate this mainly because she knew she would be losing me for almost 3 months, and the last thing she wanted to see was me hiding away in our final weeks together.

Luckily, we were able to handle the situation by discussing the issue. She let me know how she was feeling, and how my actions were causing it. I was glad that we talked, because I was so far gone that I wasn't picking up on the hints. I was able to rationalize my actions and come back. We made our final days together memorable to try and make the time apart bearable.

Here, I was primarily reminded of my nature-- I withdrawal into my head. And once you are there, it can be tough to get out without outside assistance. And for that I am thankful.
