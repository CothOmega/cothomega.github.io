Asking Good Questions

I feel like I ask good questions...when I ask them. I would say that one thing my History degree gave me was a stubbornness for research. I commonly poured over books, sites, or text to find answers. And since I would be working on something very specific, there wouldn't necessarily always be a resource where I could go to and find a straight answer. Coding is much different than that. Especially with being a newbie. The biggest things I learned in the past that continue to help now are copious details and detailed steps of things I have already done. If you can let someone know exactly what you have done so far, you can proceed forward without backtracking.

Some of the most unhelpful questions include "What did I do wrong?" and "Why is this happening?" Without any details as to what has been done so far. I mean, yes you can provide a link to your code, but tell us what you did. Has it always done this? Is there a time in which it worked? What did you change since then?? The list goes on. Be as thorough as possible! We want to help! :D
